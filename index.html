WHO AM I

By profession, I dive deep into bits and bytes — building real-time, embedded systems that power everything from aircraft to industrial energy platforms. I love the challenge of understanding a system from the inside out, making it work reliably, and improving it line by line.

But let’s not pretend I’m all work and no vibe.

I like slow bike rides with friends.Or maybe fast — depends on the mood. Not too fast though. I ride like I live — with caution, curiosity, and just enough speed to make people think I’m adventurous while I’m really just scanning the road for potholes. No destination in mind, no playlists to impress — just good company and better conversations. For me, it’s never been about where you go. It’s who you go with. That’s the whole point.

I’m the kind of person who prefers silence over small talk, and deep conversations over notifications. Loud places? Meh. Give me a breezy evening, a chair, and someone who doesn’t mind asking, “What do you think happens after this life?” — now we’re talking.

Lately, I’ve picked up photography — still figuring it out, but I enjoy the process. It reminds me of my childhood, when I used to sketch and paint… until society said, “That’s cute. Now go become an engineer.” So here I am. But the artist never really left — he just learned to debug.

So if you’re here to know what I do: I build things, I break them, I fix them better. And if you’re here to know who I am: well… I’m still figuring that part out — but at least I brought a camera. Not really. I brought a phone. With a slightly better camera. That counts, right?
